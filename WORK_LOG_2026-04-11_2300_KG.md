## Known Limitations

- The KG is a curated local demo graph, not a general web-scale extractor.
- The closed-world assumption is only valid for this demo KG and should not be treated as real-world completeness.
- Query templates are intentionally simple and rule-based; they are not a full semantic parser.
- Universal handling is approximated by retrieving explicit `not_capable_of` counterexamples from the demo KG.
- 2-hop traversal is implemented, but deeper multi-hop reasoning is not yet generalized.
- Dense and BM25 comparisons use triple/path ids as gold evidence, so they correctly demonstrate structural mismatch but do not yet support text-to-triple alignment.
- Answer generation remains scaffold-level and echoes retrieved evidence.

## Concepts and Topics Used

- RDFLib: used as the canonical local RDF triple store.
- NetworkX: used as a graph traversal mirror for 2-hop path queries.
- Triple: a fact shaped as subject, predicate, object.
- Turtle: a human-readable RDF serialization format.
- Membership lookup: checking `entity is_a class`.
- Property lookup: checking facts such as `bat has_property wings` or `whale capable_of swim`.
- Relation lookup: checking facts such as `bat eats mosquito`.
- 2-hop traversal: finding paths such as `bat -> mammal -> vertebrate`.
- Existential query: checking whether at least one example exists, such as `Can a mammal fly?`.
- Universal query: checking whether a claim applies to all known members, such as `Are all mammals able to fly?`.
- Counterexample evidence: a fact that disproves a universal claim under the demo KG.
- Closed-world demo assumption: treating the curated KG as complete only for the narrow demo slice.
